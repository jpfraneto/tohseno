# One Quiet Mark — illustrative source prompt

This prompt exists to keep the template's manifest valid. Replace it with the
owner's actual `MASTER_PROMPT.md` before building a product.

Create a private continuity application for a person who wants one small,
non-competitive acknowledgement that they returned today. The one primary
action is to press and hold a mark for one full breath, then release it. Five
seconds of active holding completes the action. Leaving the app first preserves
no meaningful content and records an interrupted attempt only after local
progress has begun.

Completion creates one immutable local event and a tiny structured artifact.
Show one quiet acknowledgement and invite another return no sooner than the
next local day. Accumulate private return dates as a sparse trace; never turn
them into a leaderboard, shame mechanism, social feed, profile, dashboard, or
generic CRUD collection.

The core loop works offline before account creation. AI, a remote service,
payment, synchronization, public proof, and external analytics are absent. The
owner may export their artifacts manually. Contextual identity and its actual
cryptographic suite must be chosen and tested before production; recovery of
identity and recovery of content remain separate.

Ask the owner before spending money, creating infrastructure, changing DNS,
rotating production credentials, or submitting to application stores. Return
all source, identifiers, credentials, environments, production URLs, and
ejection instructions to the owner.
